Mission & Strategic Vision
02 / 11

Why FitBridge exists

Mission

To help fitness users stay consistent and accountable by connecting them with their trainers through simple workout tracking, progress monitoring, and daily habit support.

Strategic Vision

To create a focused fitness platform that strengthens the relationship between trainers and users, enabling continuous guidance beyond training sessions and supporting long-term healthy habits.

Problem Statement
04 / 11

The gap between
sessions and results

📊

Fragmented tracking

Workouts, metrics & meals tracked across separate apps, notes, or messages.

🔗

Broken accountability loop

No structured system keeps users accountable between training sessions.

👁

Limited trainer visibility

Trainers can't see what users do on their own — meals, workouts, or body changes.

📉

Inconsistent body tracking

Metrics are rarely logged consistently, making it hard to show real results.

💬

Informal communication

Guidance lives in chats or memory — not a structured, actionable system.

Core Insight

Many fitness users struggle to stay consistent because communication with trainers, workout tracking, body data, and meal logging are often handled separately or informally — through messaging apps, notes, or memory.

Trainers also have limited visibility into what users actually do outside of scheduled sessions.

This leads to

Inconsistent workouts Poor accountability Missed body tracking No meal visibility Weak trainer guidance

MVP Scope
09 / 11

What's in the MVP

Core Features (In Scope)

1

Trainer Workout Logging

Trainer logs or assigns workouts directly to users in the app.

2

User Workout History

User views their complete workout history in a structured timeline.

3

Video Workout Recommendations

Trainer adds video links; user follows on off-days.

4

Body Metrics Logging

User logs weight, body fat %, and InBody data over time.

5

Meal Logging

User logs daily meals with simple text/photo input.

6

Trainer Data Review

Trainer views user's workout, meal, and body data logs.

Excluded from MVP

  • E-commerce or payments
  • Advanced AI recommendations
  • Live video training
  • Wearable integrations
  • Complex social features

MVP Goal

A simple, working prototype that allows trainers and users to stay connected through workout tracking, basic body data tracking, and meal logging.

Success Criteria & Value Hypothesis
10 / 11

How we measure success

  • Trainers can easily record or assign workouts for users
  • Users can clearly view their workout history and follow guidance
  • Users can log body metrics (weight, body fat) consistently over time
  • Users can log daily meals and share them with their trainer
  • Users can follow recommended workout videos when they miss a session
  • At least one full workflow (trainer → user → tracking → review) works in testing

Value Hypothesis

If

trainers and users are connected through a simple system for logging workouts, tracking body data, and sharing meal records…

Then

users will be more consistent and accountable…

Because

trainers can monitor progress and provide continuous guidance beyond training sessions.

Alpha Definition

The alpha version is successful when all of the following work end-to-end:

1

A trainer can log or assign a workout to a user

2

A user can view workout records and follow a suggested video

3

A user can log body metrics (weight, body fat, etc.)

4

A user can log their daily meals

5

A trainer can review all user data (workouts, meals, body metrics)

6

One complete trainer → user → tracking → review workflow runs without assistance

System Visualization · Circular Life-cycle
12 / 15
ENTITIES Circular Product
Life-cycle Visualization
Focus

Continuous loop of engagement, tracking, and improvement between clients and trainers.

Stages
  • Onboard → Sign up, set goals, connect trainer.
  • Plan → Trainer assigns workouts & videos.
  • Train → Client completes sessions, logs sets.
  • Log → Meals & body metrics recorded.
  • Review → Trainer analyzes data, sends notes.
  • Evolve → Goals updated, program adjusted.
System Visualization · Circular Life-cycle
13 / 15
ANALYSIS Circular Product
Life-cycle Visualization
Leverage Points
  • Train → Log → Review loop drives accountability and retention.
  • Trainer notes after Review re-motivate clients and tighten feedback.
  • Evolve allows program scaling — same client, higher goals over time.
Risks / Challenges
  • If Log breaks, trainer loses all visibility into client progress.
  • Plan → Train friction if workouts feel too hard — dropout risk.
  • Evolve requires trainer time — scalability concern at scale.

System Visualization · Network Map
15 / 15
ANALYSIS Network Visualization
Leverage Points
  • FitBridge App as hub — all actors pass through it, creating one accountability point.
  • Notification Engine closes the loop automatically — trainer doesn't chase clients.
  • Analytics Dashboard gives trainers data-driven insight for faster adjustments.
Risks / Challenges
  • Single hub dependency — if app goes down, all connections break.
  • Meal Log & Metrics are client-driven — low compliance breaks trainer visibility.
  • Notification overload — too many alerts may cause clients to disengage.
  • Health data privacy — stricter handling required than typical apps.
